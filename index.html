Overview

World War II was fought between 1939 and 1945 and involved many countries across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Pacific. It was primarily fought between the Allied Powers and Axis Powers and resulted in tens of millions of deaths.

Major leaders shaped the war, while technological developments, naval warfare, air combat, and armored battles transformed military history forever.

Timeline of Major Events

1939 – Germany invades Poland, beginning World War II.

1940 – Germany conquers France.

1941 – Japan attacks Pearl Harbor.

1942 – Battle of Stalingrad and Battle of Midway begin.

1943 – Axis defeats increase after Kursk.

1944 – Allied forces launch D-Day invasion.

1945 – Germany and Japan surrender, ending the war.
